    Historically, when professionals shared their insights on public platforms, their expertise was often overshadowed by unconstructive feedback, including dismissive or mocking remarks from non-experts. This created an environment where well-informed perspectives were undervalued due to uninformed opinions.To address this, we’ve designed a system that preserves the integrity of professional critiques by limiting engagement to fellow experts. This ensures that professional reviews receive thoughtful, relevant feedback from qualified critics while still allowing general users to read and learn from them.Similarly, to maintain fairness, professionals cannot comment on user-generated reviews. However, both groups can freely read each other’s perspectives, fostering knowledge-sharing while maintaining a balanced and respectful discussion space.

    While global art platforms do exist, Sri Lankan art often lacks dedicated spaces for critical engagement and discussion.By focusing exclusively on Sri Lankan artworks, we aim to provide a specialized platform that highlights and fosters deeper appreciation for the country's unique artistic heritage.If the platform were open to all global art, reviews of Sri Lankan works could become overshadowed, further limiting their visibility. Our mission is to ensure that Sri Lankan art receives the focused attention and recognition it deserves.

    While our primary focus is promoting and analyzing Sri Lankan art, users are welcome to discuss foreign artworks as well. However, our analytical tools, metrics, and featured content are specifically tailored to evaluate Sri Lankan art, and we do not track or highlight statistics for international works. For in-depth discussions on global art, other dedicated platforms may offer more comprehensive resources. Our platform remains open to both critics and general users, with separate spaces to ensure clarity of purpose while maintaining flexibility for diverse artistic interests.

    The Article Collage and Other Articles sections are dedicated spaces for publishing original articles related to arts and culture. However, they serve different purposes and have specific submission guidelines:

  • Article Collage: This section is primarily for articles focusing on Sri Lankan arts, even if the specific art category (e.g., paintings, sculptures) is not listed as a dedicated section on the website. Writers are encouraged to share well-researched and insightful content about Sri Lankan artistic traditions, modern works, or related topics.

  • Other Articles: This section is exclusively for articles discussing foreign artworks (e.g., international artists, global art movements). Submissions about Sri Lankan art are not permitted here.

  • Eligibility: Only verified professionals (e.g., critics, scholars, experienced writers) are allowed to contribute to these sections. General users cannot submit articles.
